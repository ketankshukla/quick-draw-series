## 2. SERIES OVERVIEW

- **Series Name:** Quick Draw Series
- **Genre:** Spaghetti Western / Frontier Epic
- **Fiction or Non-Fiction:** Fiction
- **Number of Books:** 5 books, 10 chapters each. 50 chapters total.
- **Words per Chapter:** ~3,000 words per chapter (STRICT)
- **Words per Book:** ~30,000 words per book
- **Total Words:** ~150,000 words total
- **Target Audience:** Adults 18-55 who love Westerns, frontier adventure, revenge sagas, and cinematic storytelling
- **Tone:** Gritty, cinematic, sun-scorched — the literary equivalent of a Sergio Leone film. Tense dialogue, explosive action, deep emotional undercurrents, and a moral landscape as vast and unforgiving as the desert itself.
- **Content Rating:** 18+ Adults (violence, romance, death)

### Series Concept

The **Quick Draw Series** is a five-book spaghetti western saga set in the Arizona Territory of the 1870s. It follows Elijah "Quick Draw" Cade, a former Union sharpshooter turned peaceful rancher whose world is destroyed when the ruthless outlaw Rattlesnake Reno Hatch murders his family as part of a corrupt railroad baron's land-grab scheme. What begins as a white-hot quest for vengeance becomes an epic tale of justice, love, sacrifice, and the death of the Old West.

Across five books, Elijah assembles an unlikely alliance — a fierce female sharpshooter with her own vendetta, a Lakota tracker who moves like smoke through the canyons, a former gunslinger turned preacher haunted by his own past, and a wisecracking trail companion who masks his courage with humor. Together they take on an outlaw empire funded by railroad money, fighting across the mesas, canyons, and dusty frontier towns of the American Southwest.

### Series Tagline

_"In the Arizona Territory, justice comes at the speed of a bullet."_

## 3. SERIES STRUCTURE

### Act I — The Reckoning (Books 1-2)

**Theme:** Loss, transformation, and the birth of a hunter.

- **Book 1:** Elijah's world is destroyed. He transforms from peaceful rancher to relentless bounty hunter, picking up his first allies and his first kills.
- **Book 2:** The pursuit leads into Devil's Canyon. Elijah meets Marisol and Solomon. Colonel Crane's railroad conspiracy is revealed.

**Act I ends with:** The discovery that Reno is merely the weapon; Colonel Crane is the hand.

### Act II — The War (Books 3-4)

**Theme:** Escalation, sacrifice, and the cost of justice.

- **Book 3:** Open war erupts. Marisol emerges as a legendary warrior. The Battle of Red Mesa devastates both sides.
- **Book 4:** The emotional heart. Love deepens, secrets are revealed, and Father Thorn's sacrifice changes everything.

**Act II ends with:** Father Thorn's death and Crane's declaration of total war.

### Act III — The Final Stand (Book 5)

**Theme:** Resolution, reckoning, and a new dawn.

- **Book 5:** The final confrontation in the ghost town of Perdition. Scorpion Vega falls. Elijah and Reno face each other for the last time.

**Act III ends with:** Elijah and Marisol ride into the sunrise. The frontier is over. The legend lives on.

## 7. BACK MATTER CONTENT

### About the Author (all books)

Harlan J. Westbrook grew up on a working cattle ranch outside Amarillo, Texas. After a riding accident ended his rodeo career at twenty-eight, he turned to writing. He spent two decades working ranches across the American Southwest, collecting campfire stories, family legends, and the quiet wisdom of men and women who never wrote anything down but knew everything that mattered. The Quick Draw Series is a love letter to the American West. He lives in Santa Fe, New Mexico.

### Also By (all books)

Quick Draw Series: Book I: Dust and Vengeance, Book II: The Devil's Canyon, Book III: Blood on the Horizon, Book IV: Ashes of the Frontier, Book V: The Last Draw. Box Sets: Act I (Books 1-2), Act II (Books 3-4), Complete Collection (Books 1-5).

### Author's Notes (unique per book)

**Book 1:** Writing Elijah's transformation from rancher to bounty hunter was the hardest thing I've put on paper. Goodness doesn't disappear — it changes shape. I grew up around quiet men who worked hard and never raised their voices. But I heard stories of what those men did when pushed too far. Those stories are in these pages.

**Book 2:** Devil's Canyon is a dozen real places I've ridden through, compressed into one impossible landscape. Introducing Marisol was a turning point — she is not a love interest who needs saving. She is a warrior who chooses her own battles.

**Book 3:** War changes everything. I didn't want a sanitized battle. The sharpshooter duel emerged from the characters themselves. Old Man Coyote came to me the same way he comes to Elijah — unexpectedly, with wisdom I didn't know I had.

**Book 4:** Gunfights are easy. Love is hard. The canyon sunset chapter took three weeks and eleven rewrites. Father Thorn's death broke my heart. He wasn't planned to die — but the story demanded it.

**Book 5:** I didn't want this series to end. The final draw lasted nine seconds — nine seconds I spent two months writing. The epilogue is the image I've been writing toward since page one. Thank you for riding this trail with me.
